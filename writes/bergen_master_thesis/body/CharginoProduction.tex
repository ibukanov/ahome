\chapter{Chargino production}

\section{Lagrangian representation}

In this chapter I consider the production cross section
for the lightest charginos by the process 
\bel{charginoProdProc}
e^+e^- \rightarrow \chi^+\chi^- ,
\ee

where I denote by $\chi$ the lightest chargino $\chi_1$. Also I will refer
to $\chi^+$ as the chargino particle and to $\chi^-$ as 
the corresponding antiparticle.

\P
At the tree level, the process is represented by the following 
interaction terms from the MSSM Lagrangian,

\bel{eToChi}
L_{int}
  = L[e, \gamma] + L[e, Z]
  + L[\chi, \gamma] + L[\chi, Z] 
  + L[e, \tilde{\nu_{e1}}, \chi]
.
\ee         

Here $L[e, \gamma]$ and $L[e, Z]$  represent the usual coupling 
of electrons with photons and $Z^0$ bosons and the remaining terms are given 
by~\rf{charginoCoupling} and~\rf{ChiLSNu}. 

\P
To simplify the cross-section calculations I found it useful to introduce 
the following notations,

\bem
L[e, \gamma] & = & -\overline{\psi}_e e\gu{\rho} \psi_e A_\rho
\nel
&\equiv&
\overline{\psi}_e \gu{\rho}(a_\gamma - b_\gamma \gd5 ) \psi_e D^\gamma_\rho,
\nel
L[e, Z] &=& - \frac{g}{4\cos\theta_W} 
  \overline{\psi}_e \gu{\rho} [(1 - 4\sin^2\theta_W) - \gd5] \psi_e Z_\rho
\nel
& \equiv &
\overline{\psi}_e \gu{\rho}(a_Z - b_Z \gd5 ) \psi_e D^Z_\rho,

\ee

with
\bel{chargeGabIs}
a_\gamma = -e \equiv - \abs{e}, \quad b_\gamma = 0, 
\ee

\beml{chargeZabIs}
a_Z  =  -\frac{g}{4\cos\theta_W}(1 - 4\sin^2\theta_W),
\quad 
b_Z  =  -\frac{g}{4\cos\theta_W},
\ee

and
\be
D^\gamma_\rho =  A_\rho, \quad D^Z_\rho =  Z_\rho
.
\ee

According to~\rf{charginoCoupling} I can write 
in the case of the  coupling of the lightest chargino $\chi_1$
with the gauge bosons
\beml{charge-L-with-chi-and-gamma}
L[\chi, \gamma] &=& 
\overline{\psi}_{\chi_1} e\gu{\rho} \psi_{\chi_1} A_\rho
\nel
& \equiv &
\overline{\psi}_{\chi}\gu{\rho}(A_\gamma - B_\gamma \gu5)\psi_{\chi}
   D^\gamma_\rho,
\nel
L[\chi,Z] &=&
    \frac{g}{4 \cos \theta_W} 
    \overline{\psi}_{\chi_1} \gu{\rho} 
\nel && \times
    \Bigl\lbrace 
          [2\cos (2\theta_W) 
            + \abs{Y_{11}}^2 + \abs{X_{11}}^2]
        
          + \gu5 [\abs{Y_{11}}^2 - \abs{X_{11}}^2]\Bigr\rbrace

    \psi_{\chi_1} Z_\rho
\nel
& \equiv &
  \overline{\psi}_{\chi}\gu{\rho}(A_Z - B_Z \gu5)\psi_{\chi} 
      D^Z_\rho,
\ee

where 

\bel{chargeGABIs}
A_\gamma  = e, \quad B_\gamma =   0, 
\ee

and
\beml{chargeZABIs}
A_Z &=& \frac{g}{4 \cos \theta_W}
             [2\cos (2\theta_W) + \abs{Y_{11}}^2 + \abs{X_{11}}^2],
\nel
B_Z &=& - \frac{g}{4 \cos \theta_W} [\abs{Y_{11}}^2 - \abs{X_{11}}^2].
\ee

The interaction term between electrons and positrons, charginos and
scalar electron neutrinos is given by~\rf{lagranChiLSNu}
(I write in the following $\tilde\nu^L_e$ simply as $\tilde\nu$)

\bem
L[e, \tilde{\nu}_{e1}, \chi] & = &
  \frac{g}{2} 
    [-\psi^T_{\chi_1}C (\Vc^\chi_{e1L} + \Ac^\chi_{e1L}\gd{5}) \psi_{e}
                    \tilde{\nu}^\hc
       + \overline{\psi}_e (\Vc^{\chi\hc}_{e1L} - \Ac^{\chi\hc}_{e1L} \gd{5})
                 C^{-1}\overline{\psi}_{\chi_1}^T \tilde{\nu}]
\nel
& \equiv &
        -\psi^T_{\chi}C (F + G\gd{5}) \psi_e
                    \cc{\tilde{\nu}}
       + \overline{\psi}_e (F^\hc - G^\hc \gd{5})
                 C^{-1}\overline{\psi}_{\chi}^T \tilde{\nu}.
\ee

Here
\be
F = \frac{g}{2}\Vc^\chi_{e1L},
\quad 
G  =  \frac{g}{2}\Ac^\chi_{e1L}
.
\ee

Under the approximation 
\be
\abs{Y_{i2}\frac{m_e}{\sqrt{2} m_W \cos\beta}}
\le \frac{m_e}{\sqrt{2} m_W \cos\beta} \approx 0
\ee

($\abs{Y_{i2}} \le 1$ as an element of the unitary matrix $Y$), 
the coefficients $F$ and $G$ become
\bel{chargeFandG1}
F \approx -{g \over 2} X_{11}^\hc,
\quad
G  \approx {g \over 2} X_{11}^\hc
\ee

\P
The expressions for
$X_{11}^\hc$, $\abs{X_{11}}^2$ and $\abs{Y_{11}}^2$ are given by~\rf{lagXandY}, 
\bel{chargeFandG2}
X_{11}^\hc = \cos\theta_X, 
\quad 
\abs{X_{11}}^2 = \cos^2\theta_X,
\quad 
\abs{Y_{11}}^2 = \cos^2\theta_Y
,
\ee

\P
With this notations $L_{int}$ becomes

\bel{charge-useful-form-for-L}
L_{int} = \sum_{k = \gamma, Z} (L_e^k + L_\chi^k) 
            + L_{\tilde\nu} + L_{\tilde\nu}^\hc,
\ee

where I set
\beml{charge-useful-form-for-L-2}
L_e^k & = & 
\overline{\psi}_{e}\gu{\rho}(a_k - b_k \gu5)\psi_{e}D^k_\rho
,
\nel
L_\chi^k & = & 
\overline{\psi}_{\chi}\gu{\rho}(A_k - B_k \gu5)\psi_{\chi}D^k_\rho
,
\nel 
L_{\tilde\nu} & = &  \overline{\psi}_e (F^\hc - G^\hc \gd{5})
                 C^{-1}\overline{\psi}_{\chi}^T \tilde{\nu}
,                 
\nel
L_{\tilde\nu}^\hc & = & -\psi^T_{\chi}C (F + G\gd{5}) \psi_e \tilde{\nu}^\hc
.
\ee


\section{Production Amplitude}

According to~\cite{SMTextBook} the tree-level transition amplitude 
for the process \rf{charginoProdProc}
is given by the second-order $S$-matrix element, 

\bem
\lefteqn{
S_2(e^+e^- \rightarrow \chi^+\chi^-) = 
\frac{(-i)^2}{2!} \int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x' 
}
\nel &&{} \times 
\bra{\chi^+(\ppa, \rpa), \chi^-(\ppb, \rpb)}
  \Torder\lbrace 
    \Norder[L_{int}(x)] \Norder[L_{int}(x')]\rbrace
\ket{e^+(\pa, \ra), e^-(\pb, \rb)}
.
\ee

Here $\pa$, $\ra$, $\pb$, $\rb$ denote the initial momentum and polarization
state for the positron and electron fields, 
$\ppa$, $\rpa$, $\ppb$, $\rpb$ are used
for the final states of antichargino and chargino.
The functional $\Torder$ gives 
the time ordered product of its arguments
and $\Norder[L_{int}]$ stands for the normal-ordered form 
of the interaction Lagrangian. 
 
\P

I must note here that there is an ambiguity in the definition of
a multi-fermion state. 
For example, for any two fermions $f_1$, $f_2$
one can take either 
\bel{charge-multi-fermion-state-is}
\ket{f_1, f_2} 
    = (2\pi)^{3\over 2} c^\hc(f_1) (2\pi)^{3\over 2} c^\hc(f_2)\ket{0} 
    = (2\pi)^3 c^\hc(f_1) c^\hc(f_2)\ket{0} 
    
\ee

or
\be
\ket{f_1, f_2} 
    = (2\pi)^{3\over 2} c^\hc(f_2) (2\pi)^{3\over 2} c^\hc(f_1)\ket{0} 
    = -(2\pi)^3 c^\hc(f_1)c^\hc(f_2)\ket{0}.
\ee
where the factor $(2\pi)^{3\over 2}$ reflects the unit volume normalization of 
the states.

\P
This causes an uncertainty in the sign of the S matrix for processes 
with more than one fermion in the initial or final state
although measurable quantities do not have this ambiguity.
Nevertheless, from the computational point of view the sign should be fixed
somehow. Thus I choose the first possibility~\rf{charge-multi-fermion-state-is} and

\bem
\bra{f_1, f_2} & \equiv & \left(\ket{f_1, f_2}\right)^\hc 
    = \left((2\pi)^3 c^\hc(f_1)c^\hc(f_2)\ket{0}\right)^\hc 
\nel & = &
    (2\pi)^3 \bra{0}c(f_2)c(f_1)
\ee

\P
I may write now for the initial and final states of 
the process~\rf{charginoProdProc},
\bem
\bra{\chi^+(\ppa, \rpa), \chi^-(\ppb, \rpb)}
&=& 
(2\pi)^3 \bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa),
\nel
\ket{e^+(\pa, \ra), e^-(\pb, \rb)} 
&= &
(2\pi)^3 d^\hc_e(\pa,\ra)c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0},
\ee

where $c$ and $d$ are fermion and antifermion annihilation operators 
and $c^\hc$ and $d^\hc$ are creation operators. 

\P
The explicit form~\rf{charge-useful-form-for-L} of $L_{int}$ 
and the commutational and anticommutational properties of different fields
it depends on give

\bem
\lefteqn{
S_2 = \frac{(-i)^2}{2!} \int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x' 
    \bra{\chi^+(\ppa, \rpa), \chi^-(\ppb, \rpb)}
}
\nel
&& {} \times
\Torder\Bigl\lbrace 
  \sum_{k = \gamma, Z} \bigl( 
    \Norder[L_e^k(x)]\Norder[L_\chi^k(x)(x')] 
    +  \Norder[L_\chi^k(x)(x)]\Norder[L_e^k(x')] \bigr)
\nel && \qquad \qquad {}
+ \Norder[L_{\tilde\nu}(x)]\Norder[L_{\tilde\nu}^\hc(x')] 
+ \Norder[L_{\tilde\nu}^\hc(x)]\Norder[L_{\tilde\nu}(x')] 
 \Bigr\rbrace
\nel && \times
\ket{e^+(\pa, \ra), e^-(\pb, \rb)}


\nel
& = & -\int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x' 
\bra{\chi^+(\ppa, \rpa), \chi^-(\ppb, \rpb)}
\nel && {} \times
  \Torder\Bigl\{ 
  \sum_{k = \gamma, Z} \Norder[L_e^k(x)]\Norder[L_\chi^k(x)(x')]
    + \Norder[L_{\tilde\nu}^\hc(x)]\Norder[L_{\tilde\nu}(x')]
 \Bigr\}
\nel && \times
\ket{e^+(\pa, \ra), e^-(\pb, \rb)}
,
\ee

where I have taken into account that 
the terms like $\Norder[L(x)]$ here contains an even number (two)
of fermion fields so I can freely move them to any position 
in the $\Torder$-product. Thus if I simply rename $x \leftrightarrow x'$
and change the integration order, I find, for example, 
\bem
\int\! d^4\!x\, d^4\!x' 
  \Torder\Bigl\lbrace 
     \Norder[L_\chi^k(x)(x)]\Norder[L_e^k(x')]
\Bigr\rbrace
& = &
\int\! d^4\!x\, d^4\!x' 
  \Torder\Bigl\lbrace 
     \Norder[L_e^k(x')]\Norder[L_\chi^k(x)(x)]
\Bigr\rbrace
\nel
& = &
\int\! d^4\!x'\, d^4\!x
  \Torder\Bigl\lbrace 
     \Norder[L_e^k(x)]\Norder[L_\chi^k(x')(x)]
 \Bigr\rbrace
\nel
& = &
\int\! d^4\!x\, d^4\!x'
  \Torder\Bigl\lbrace 
     \Norder[L_e^k(x)]\Norder[L_\chi^k(x')(x)]
 \Bigr\rbrace
, 
\ee

and in the same way
\bem
\int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x' 
    \Norder[L_{\tilde\nu}(x)]\Norder[L_{\tilde\nu}^\hc(x')] 
& = &
\int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x' 
    \Norder[L_{\tilde\nu}^\hc(x)]\Norder[L_{\tilde\nu}(x')] 
.
\ee

At this stage 
I can represent $S_2$ according to~\rf{charge-useful-form-for-L-2} 
and~\rf{charge-multi-fermion-state-is} as, 

\bel{charge-S2-as-two-term}
S_2 = \sum_{k = \gamma, Z} S_k + S_{\tilde\nu},
\ee

with
\beml{charge-S-D-k-is}
S_k & = & -(2\pi)^6 \int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x' 
\bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
\nel && \times
    \Torder\Bigl\lbrace 
        \Norder[\overline\psi_{\chi} 
                \gu{\rho}(A_k - B_k\gd{5})
                \psi_{\chi}
                D_\rho]_{x}
        \Norder[\overline\psi_e 
                \gu{\sigma}(a_k - b_k\gd{5})
                \psi_e
                D_\sigma]_{x'}
    \Bigr\rbrace 
\nel && \times 
    d^\hc_e(\pa,\ra)c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0}
,
\ee

and
\beml{charge-S-nu-is}
S_{\tilde\nu} & = & (2\pi)^6 \int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x' 
\bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
\nel && \times
    \Torder\Bigl\lbrace 
        \Norder[\psi^T_{\chi}C(F + G\gd{5})
                \psi_{e}\cc{\tilde\nu}]_x
        \Norder[\overline{\psi}_e (F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})
                C^{-1}\overline{\psi}_{\chi}^T{\tilde\nu}]_{x'} 
    \Bigr\rbrace 
\nel && \times 
    d^\hc_e(\pa,\ra)c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0}
.
\ee


\P
I follow the usual approach and calculate $S_2$ by the means of
Wick's theorem. According to~\rf{gammaFermionField} I have,
\bem
\lefteqn{
\psi(x) \; = \; \posPart\psi(x) + \negPart\psi(x)
}
\nel & = &
\sum_{r = +, -}\int d^3p \left(\frac{m}{(2\pi)^3 E_p}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}
    [ u_r(p)c_r(p) e^{-ipx} + v_r(p)d_r^\hc(p) e^{ipx} ] ,
\nel
\lefteqn{
\overline{\psi}(x) \; = \;  
\overline{\posPart\psi}(x) + \overline{\negPart\psi}(x)
}
\nel & = &
\sum_{r = +, -}\int d^3p \left(\frac{m}{(2\pi)^3 E_p}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}
    [ \ub_r(p)c_r^\hc(p) e^{ipx} + \vb_r(p)d_r(p) e^{-ipx} ]
    ,
\ee

where $u_r(p) = u_r(p/m)$ and $v_r(p) = v_r(p/m)$.

\P
Thus I obtain for first term $S_k$ in \rf{charge-S2-as-two-term} 
(I suppress here the index $k$) :
\beml{charge-ref11}
S_k & = & -(2\pi)^6\int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x'
\bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
\nel &&{} \qquad\times 
\Torder\Bigl\lbrace 
            \Norder[\overline\psi_{\chi} 
                    \gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})
                    \psi_{\chi}
                    D_\rho]_{x}
            \Norder[\overline\psi_e 
                    \gu{\sigma}(a - b\gd{5})
                    \psi_e
                    D_\sigma]_{x'}
        \Bigr\rbrace
\nel &&{} \qquad\times 
    d^\hc_e(\pa,\ra)c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0}
\nel & = & 
-(2\pi)^6 \int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x'
    \bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
\nel &&{} \qquad\times 
        \Torder\Bigl\lbrace 
            [\overline{\posPart\psi}_{\chi} 
                    \gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})
                    \negPart\psi_{\chi}
                    D_\rho]_{x}
            [\overline{\negPart\psi}_e 
                    \gu{\sigma}(a - b\gd{5})
                    \posPart\psi_e
                    D_\sigma]_{x'}
        \Bigr\rbrace
\nel &&{} \qquad\times 
    d^\hc_e(\pa,\ra)c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0}
\nel
& = & 
-(2\pi)^6 \int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x'
    \bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
\nel &&{} \qquad\times 
        \Norder\Bigl\lbrace 
            [\overline{\posPart\psi}_{\chi} 
                    \gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})
                    \negPart\psi_{\chi}]_{x}
            [\overline{\negPart\psi}_e 
                    \gu{\sigma}(a - b\gd{5})
                    \posPart\psi_e]_{x'}
        \Bigr\rbrace    
            iD_{\rho\sigma}(x' - x)
\nel &&{} \qquad\times 
    d^\hc_e(\pa,\ra)c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0},
\ee

where $D_{\rho\sigma}(x' - x)$ stands for $\gamma$ or $Z^0$
propagator. Normal ordering of the fermion operators in~\rf{charge-ref11} 
gives 

\beml{charge-ref11-a}
S_k
& = & 
-(2\pi)^6 \int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x'
    \bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
\nel &&{} \qquad\times 
        [\overline{\negPart\psi}_e 
                \gu{\sigma}(a - b\gd{5})
                \posPart\psi_e]_{x'}
        [\overline{\posPart\psi}_{\chi} 
                \gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})
                \negPart\psi_{\chi}]_{x}
        iD_{\rho\sigma}(x' - x)
\nel &&{} \qquad\times 
    d^\hc_e(\pa,\ra)c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0} .
\ee

From the anticommutational relations between fermion operators 
and~\rf{gammaFermionField} I obtain for the scalar product in~\rf{charge-ref13}:
\beml{charge-ref12}
\lefteqn{
\bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
[\overline{\negPart\psi}_e\dots \posPart\psi_e]_{x'} \}
[\overline{\posPart\psi}_{\chi} ... \negPart\psi_{\chi}]_{x}
d^\hc_e(\pa,\ra)c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0}
} \;
\nel & = &
-
\left(\frac{m_e^2}{\Ea\Eb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
\bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa) 
[\overline{\posPart\psi}_{\chi} ... \negPart\psi_{\chi}]_{x} \ket{0}
\nel && \qquad \times
[\vb_{\ra}(\pa)e^{-i\pa x'} \dots u_{\rb}(\pb)e^{-i\pb x'}] 

\nel & = & 
-
\left(\frac{m_e^2 m_\chi^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
\dirProd{0}{0}
\nel && \quad \times
[\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)e^{i\ppb x} \dots v_{\rpa}(\ppa)e^{i\ppa x}]
[\vb_{\ra}(\pa)e^{-i\pa x'} \dots u_{\rb}(\pb)e^{-i\pb x'}] 
,
\ee

where $\Ea$ and $\Eb$ are the energies of the incoming particles and 
$\Epa$ and $\Epb$ are the energies of the outgoing ones. In the 
center-of-mass frame one will have $\Ea = \Eb$ and $\Epa = \Epb$.

\P
Thus from the equations~\rf{charge-ref12} I find

\beml{charge-S2-boson-tmp1}
S_k 
& = & 
\left(\frac{m_e^2m_{\chi}^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
\int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x'
    \dirProd{0}{0}
\nel & & \qquad \times \,
    [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)e^{i\ppb x}
        \gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})
         v_{\rpa}(\ppa)e^{i\ppa x}]
\nel & & \qquad \times  \,
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)e^{-i\pa x'}
        \gu{\sigma}(a - b\gd{5})
        u_{\rb}(\pb)e^{-i\pb x'}]
    iD_{\rho\sigma}(x' - x) 
.
\ee

To calculate the integral in~\rf{charge-S2-boson-tmp1} 
I use the Fourier transformation to write
\be
iD_{\rho\sigma}(x' - x) = (2\pi)^{-4}
\int  d^4\!k\, e^{-ik(x' - x)} iD_{\rho\sigma}(k) 
.
\ee

It gives for~\rf{charge-S2-boson-tmp1}
\beml{charge-boson-S-is}
S_k & = & 
\left(\frac{m_e^2m_{\chi}^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
(2\pi)^{-4}
\int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x' d^4\!k\,
    e^{ix(\ppa + \ppb)}e^{-ix'(\pa + \pb)}e^{-ik(x' - x)}
    iD_{\rho\sigma}(k) 
\nel & & \times\,
    [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gu{\sigma}(a - b\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
\nel
& = & 
(2\pi)^{4}
\left(\frac{m_e^2m_{\chi}^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
\int d^4\!k\,
    \delta^4(k + \ppa + \ppb)\delta^4(k + \pa + \pb)
    iD_{\rho\sigma}(k) 
\nel & & \times\,
    [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gu{\sigma}(a - b\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
\nel
& = & 
(2\pi)^{4}
\left(\frac{m_e^2m_{\chi}^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
\delta^4(\ppa + \ppb - \pa - \pb) 
\left. iD_{\rho\sigma}(k) \right|_{k = - \pa - \pb =  - \ppa - \ppb} 
\nel & & \times\,
    [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gu{\sigma}(a - b\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    .
\ee

\P
Handling of the $S_{\tilde\nu}$ term 
in~\rf{charge-S2-as-two-term} is slightly
different due to the presence of the charge conjugation operation.
 
\beml{charge-ref13}
S_{\tilde\nu} & = & (2\pi)^6 \int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x'
\bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
\nel&&\qquad {} \times
       \Torder\Bigl\lbrace 
            \Norder[\psi^T_{\chi}C(F + G\gd{5})
                    \psi_{e}{\tilde\nu}^\hc]_x
            \Norder[\overline{\psi}_e (\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})
                    C^{-1}\overline{\psi}_{\chi}^T\tilde{\nu}]_{x'} 
        \Bigr\rbrace
\nel&&\qquad {} \times
    d^\hc_e(\pa,\ra)c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0}
\nel
& = & 
(2\pi)^6\int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x'
    \bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
\nel&&\qquad {} \times
        \Torder\Bigl\lbrace 
            [(\negPart{\psi})^T_{\chi}C(F + G\gd{5})
                    \posPart{\psi}_{e}\cc{\tilde{\nu}}]_x
            [\overline{\negPart{\psi}}_e (\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})
                    C^{-1}(\overline{\posPart{\psi}}_{\chi})^T\tilde{\nu}]_{x'} 
        \Bigr\rbrace
\nel & & \qquad \times \,
    d^\hc_e(\pa,\ra)c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0}
\nel
& = & 
(2\pi)^6 \int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x'
    \bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
\nel&&\qquad {} \times
        \Norder\Bigl\lbrace 
            [(\negPart{\psi})^T_{\chi}C(F + G\gd{5})
                    \posPart{\psi}_{e}]_x
            [\overline{\negPart{\psi}}_e (\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})
                    C^{-1}(\overline{\posPart{\psi}}_{\chi})^T]_{x'} 
        \Bigr\rbrace
\nel&&\qquad {} \times
        i\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(x' - x)
    d^\hc_e(\pa,\ra)c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0},
\ee

where $\Delta_{\tilde\nu}$ is the sneutrino propagator that has the usual
form of the propagator for a scalar particle. If I take into account 
that $\psi_\chi$ anticommutes with $\psi_e$ 
I will find from~\rf{gammaFermionField} in a way similar to~\rf{charge-ref12} 
that
\bem
\lefteqn{
\bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
\Norder\Bigl\lbrace 
  [(\negPart{\psi})^T_{\chi} \dots \posPart{\psi}_{e}]_x
  [\overline{\negPart{\psi}}_e \dots (\overline{\posPart{\psi}}_{\chi})^T]_{x'}
\Bigr\rbrace
d^\hc_e(\pa,\ra)c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0}
}
\nel
& = &
-
\left(\frac{m_e}{\Ea}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}
\bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
\nel && \qquad{}\times
\Norder\Bigl\lbrace 
  [(\negPart{\psi})^T_{\chi} \dots \posPart{\psi}_{e}]_x
  e^{-i\pa x'}[\vb_{\ra}(\pa) \dots (\overline{\posPart{\psi}}_{\chi})^T]_{x'}
\Bigr\rbrace
c^\hc_e(\pb,\rb)\ket{0}
\nel
& = &
\left(\frac{m_e^2}{\Ea\Eb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}
\bra{0}c_{\chi}(\ppb,\rpb)d_{\chi}(\ppa, \rpa)
\nel && \qquad{}\times
\Norder\Bigl\lbrace 
  [(\negPart{\psi})^T_{\chi} \dots u_{\rb}(\pb)]_xe^{-i\pb x}
  e^{-i\pa x'}[\vb_{\ra}(\pa) \dots (\overline{\posPart{\psi}}_{\chi})^T]_{x'}
\Bigr\rbrace
\ket{0}
\nel
& = &
\left(\frac{m_e^2 m_\chi^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}
\dirProd{0}{0}
\nel && \qquad{}\times
e^{i\ppa x}[v^T(\ppa, \rpa) \dots u_{\rb}(\pb)]e^{-i\pb x}
  e^{-i\pa x'}[\vb_{\ra}(\pa) \dots \ub^T(\ppb, \rpb)]e^{i\ppb x'}
.
\ee

Thus~\rf{charge-ref13} gives

\bem
S_{\tilde\nu} & = & 
\left(\frac{m_e^2m_{\chi}^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
\int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x'
    \dirProd{0}{0}  i\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(x' - x)
\nel & & {}\times 
    e^{i\ppa x}e^{-i\pb x}
    [{v^T(\ppa, \rpa)}C(F + G\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
\nel & & {}\times 
    e^{-i\pa x'}e^{i\ppb x'}
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)(\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})
                    C^{-1}\ub^T(\ppb, \rpb)] 
.                    
\ee

The Fourier transformation of $i\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(x' - x)$ leads to
\bem
S_{\tilde\nu}
& = &
\left(\frac{m_e^2m_{\chi}^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
(2\pi)^{-4}
\int d^4\!x\, d^4\!x' d^4\!k\,
    e^{ix(\ppa - \pb)}e^{ix'(\ppb - \pa)}e^{-ik(x' - x)}
    i\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(k)
\nel & & \qquad \times\,
    [{v^T(\ppa, \rpa)}C(F + G\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)(\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})
        C^{-1}\ub^T(\ppb, \rpb)]
\nel
& = & 
(2\pi)^{4}
\left(\frac{m_e^2m_{\chi}^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
\int d^4\!k\,
    \delta^4(\ppa - \pb + k)\delta^4(\ppb - \pa - k)
    i\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(k)
\nel & & \qquad \times\,
    [{v^T(\ppa, \rpa)}C(F + G\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)(\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})
        C^{-1}\ub^T(\ppb, \rpb)]
\nel
& = & 
(2\pi)^{4}
\left(\frac{m_e^2m_{\chi}^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
\delta^4(\ppa + \ppb - \pa - \pb) 
\left. i\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(k) \right|_{k = \pb - \ppa =  \ppb - \pa} 
\nel & & \qquad \times\,
    [{v^T(\ppa, \rpa)}C(F + G\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)(\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})
        C^{-1}\ub^T(\ppb, \rpb)]
.
\ee

Now, if I use~\rf{gammaSpinorRelation}, I will obtain
$v^T(p, s) C = - \ub$, $C^{-1}\ub^T = v$,
\bem
S_{\tilde\nu} & = & 
-(2\pi)^{4}
\left(\frac{m_e^2m_{\chi}^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
\delta(\ppa + \ppb - \pa - \pb) 
\left. i\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(k) \right|_{k = \pb - \ppa =  \ppb - \pa} 
\nel & & \quad \times\,
    [{\ub_{\rpa}(\ppa)}(F + G\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)(\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})v_{\rpb}(\ppb)]
.
\ee

So the whole amplitude can be represented in the standard form,
\beml{charginoProductionAmpl}
\lefteqn{
S_2(e^+e^- \rightarrow \chi^+\chi^-) \; = \;
\sum_{k = \gamma, Z} S_k + S_{\tilde\nu}
}
\nel
& = &
i(2\pi)^{4}
\left(\frac{m_e^2m_{\chi}^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
\delta(\ppa + \ppb - \pa - \pb) 
\nel & & {}\times
\Bigl\lbrace 

\sum_{k = \gamma, Z}  
D^k_{\rho\sigma}(\pa + \pb)  
\nel & & {}\qquad\qquad \times
    [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A_k - B_k\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gu{\sigma}(a_k - b_k\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]

\nel & & \quad {}

-
\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(\pb - \ppa)
    [\ub_{\rpa}(\ppa)(F + G\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)(\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})v_{\rpb}(\ppb)]
\Bigr\rbrace

\nel 
& \equiv & -i(2\pi)^{4}
\left(\frac{m_e^2m_{\chi}^2}{\Ea\Eb\Epa\Epb}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} 
\delta(\ppa + \ppb - \pa - \pb)M_{e\chi},
\ee

where I set
\beml{charge-M-is}
M_{e\chi} & = & \sum_{k = \gamma, Z} M_k + M_{\tilde\nu},
\ee

and
\beml{charge-M-channels-is}
M_k & = &
-D^k_{\rho\sigma}(\pa + \pb)  
\nel&&{} \quad\times
    [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A_k - B_k\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gu{\sigma}(a_k - b_k\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    ,
\nel
M_{\tilde\nu}
&= &\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(\pb - \ppa)
    [\ub_{\rpa}(\ppa)(F + G\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)(\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})v_{\rpb}(\ppb)]
    .
\nel    
\ee


In the unitary gauge the photon and $Z$ propagators have the 
form~\cite{SMTextBook}
\bel{photonPropogator}
D^\gamma_{\rho\sigma}(k) 
= - {g_{\rho\sigma}\over k^2} 
= (-g_{\rho\sigma} + \omega_\gamma k_\rho k_\sigma ) \Delta_\gamma(k^2),
\ee
\be
\omega_\gamma = 0, 
\qquad \Delta_\gamma(k^2) = {1 \over k^2},
\ee

\be
D^Z_{\rho\sigma}(k) = 
{(-g_{\rho\sigma} + k_\rho k_\sigma/\mB^2)\over
k^2 - \mB^2 + i\mB\Gamma_Z}
= (-g_{\rho\sigma} + \omega_Z k_\rho k_\sigma ) \Delta_Z(k^2),
\ee

\bel{ZPropogator}
\omega_Z = {1 \over \mB^2}, 
\qquad \Delta_Z(k^2) = {1 \over k^2 - \mB^2 + i\mB\Gamma_Z},
\ee

where $\mB$ is the mass of $Z$ and $\Gamma_\B$ is its decay rate, 
it reflects the instability of $Z$.

I also include the decay term to the sneutrino propagator,
\bel{chargeSneutrinoDelta}
\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(k) = 
\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(k^2) = 
{1 \over k^2 - m_{\tilde{\nu}}^2 + im_{\tilde{\nu}}\Gamma_{\tilde{\nu}}}
.
\ee

The delta function in \rf{charginoProductionAmpl} implies
the usual conservation law $\pa + \pb = \ppa + \ppb$.

\P
Now with the help of \rf{gammaPOnSpinors}, $\pc u = m u$ and $\pc v = -m v$,
I find from $k = \pa + \pb$ applied to the electron current
\bem
\lefteqn{
k_\sigma [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gu{\sigma}(a_k - b_k\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
\; = \; [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\slsh{k}(a_k - b_k\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
}
\nel & = &
[\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\pca(a_k - b_k\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
+
[\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\pcb(a_k - b_k\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)] 
\nel & = &
[\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\pca(a_k - b_k\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
+
[\vb_{\ra}(\pa)(a_k + b_k\gd{5})\pcb u_{\rb}(\pb)] 
\nel & = &
-[\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\ma(a_k - b_k\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
+
[\vb_{\ra}(\pa)(a_k + b_k\gd{5}) \mb u_{\rb}(\pb)] 
\nel & = &
2m_eb_k[\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gd{5}u_{\rb}(\pb)]
.
\ee

In the same way from $k = \ppa + \ppb$ I have 
\bem
\lefteqn{
k_\rho [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A_k - B_k\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
}
\nel & = &
[\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\mpb(A_k - B_k\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
-
[\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)(A_k + B_k\gd{5})\mpa v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
\nel & = &
-2m_\chi B_k[\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gd{5}v_{\rpa}(\ppa)] 
.
\ee

Thus

\beml{chargeMBoson}
M_k & = &
\Delta_k(s)\bigl\{  
    [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A_k - B_k\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gd{\rho}(a_k - b_k\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
\nel &&
{} + 4m_e m_\chi \omega_k b_k B_k 
  [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gd{5}v_{\rpa}(\ppa)][\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gd{5}u_{\rb}(\pb)] 
  \bigr\}
  ,  
\ee

and 
\beml{chargeMTriple}
M_{\tilde\nu}  &=& \Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(t)
    [\ub_{\rpa}(\ppa)(F + G\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)(\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})v_{\rpb}(\ppb)]
    ,
\ee

with
\be
s = (\pa + \pb)^2 = (\ppa + \ppb)^2, 
\qquad
t = (\ppb - \pa)^2 = (\pb - \ppa)^2
.
\ee

It is expected that the lightest chargino mass would not exceed
the order of some TeV. So I may use the approximation 
\be
m_e m_\chi \omega_Z = {m_e m_\chi \over \mB^2} \ll 1
.
\ee

At the same time I have from \rf{gammaProj} that the spinors $u$, $v$
do not depend on masses. Thus under the approximation
\be
{m_e m_\chi \over \mB^2} \approx 0
\ee
I may write, 

\bel{chargeMBosonApprox}
M_k \approx
\Delta_k(s)  
    [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A_k - B_k\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gd{\rho}(a_k - b_k\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    .
\ee

\section{Cross section}

From the usual form of \rf{charginoProductionAmpl} 
according to appendix \rf{CrossSectionApp}
I may express the center-of-mass differential and 
total cross sections for the process \rf{charginoProdProc}
through $X_{e\chi}$ that reads
\beml{chargeFullX}
X_{e\chi}
&=&  {1 \over 4}\sum_\ra\sum_\rb\sum_\rpa\sum_\rpb
   M_{e\chi}^\hc M_{e\chi}
= \frac{1}{4}\sum_{\ra, \rb, \rpa, \rpb}
\abs{\sum_{k = \gamma, Z} M_k + M_{\tilde\nu}}^2
\nel
&=& 
\sum_{k, k' = \gamma, Z} X_{kk'} + X_{\tilde\nu\tilde\nu}
 + 2\sum_{k = \gamma, Z} X_{k\tilde\nu}
.
\ee

Here the contribution from boson ($\gamma$ and $Z^0$) reaction 
channels is given by
\beml{charge-boson-X-definition}
X_{kk'} & = &
\frac{1}{4}\sum_{\ra, \rb, \rpa, \rpb} \Re(M_k M_{k'}^\hc).
\ee

and similarly the $\tilde\nu$ channel is given by
\beml{charge-sneutrino-X-definition}
X_{\tilde\nu\tilde\nu} & = &
\frac{1}{4}\sum_{\ra, \rb, \rpa, \rpb} M_{\tilde\nu} M_{\tilde\nu}^{\hc}
.
\ee

The interference term between boson and sneutrino channels has the form
\beml{charge-interference-X-definition}
X_{k\tilde\nu} & = &
\frac{1}{4}\sum_{\ra, \rb, \rpa, \rpb} \Re(M_k M_{\tilde\nu}^{\hc}) .
\ee

\P

To represent the calculations of the terms in \rf{chargeFullX}
in a compact form I suppress the index $k$ in 
the expressions~\rf{chargeMTriple} and \rf{chargeMBosonApprox},

\bel{charge-M-k-form}
M_k = \Delta_k(s)  
    [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gd{\rho}(a - b\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)].
\ee

So $M_{k'}^\hc$ is given by
\bem
M_{k'}^\hc  &=& \Delta_{k'}^\hc (s)  
    [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A' - B'\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]^\hc
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gd{\rho}(a' - b'\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]^\hc
\nel & = &     
\Delta_{k'}^\hc (s)  
    [\vb_{\rpa}(\ppa)(A' + B'\gd{5})\gu{\sigma}u_{\rpb}(\ppb)]
    [\ub_{\rb}(\pb)(a' + b'\gd{5})\gd{\sigma}v_{\ra}(\pa)], 
\ee

In the same way I find $M_{\tilde\nu}^{\hc}$

\beml{chargeMShortExp}
M_{\tilde\nu}^{\hc}
&=& \Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}^\hc(t)
    [\ub_{\rpa}(\ppa)(F + G\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]^\hc
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})v_{\rpb}(\ppb)]^\hc   
\nel & = &
 \Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}^\hc(t)
    [\ub_{\rb}(\pb)(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})u_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\rpb}(\ppb)(F + G\gd{5})v_{\ra}(\pa)]
,
\ee

where I used \rf{gammaBProperties} to calculate the hermitian conjugated 
expression.

\subsection{Contribution from boson channels}

By using the spin polarization sum formulae  \rf{gammaSpinPolarization},
\beml{SpinSums}
\sum_r {\v_k}(p, r){\vb_l}(p, r) & = & 
 \frac{1}{2m}(\slsh{p} - m)_{kl} 
    = \frac{1}{2}(\slsh{q} - 1)_{kl},
\nel
\sum_r {\u_k}(p, r){\ub_l}(p, r) & = & 
\frac{1}{2m}(\slsh{p} + m)_{kl} 
   = \frac{1}{2}(\slsh{q} + 1)_{kl},
\quad q \equiv p/m,
\ee

where $k$ and $l$ denote spinor indices , I have

\bel{charge-X-boson-1}
X_{kk'} = 
\frac{1}{4}\Re\left[\sum_{\ra, \rb, \rpa, \rpb}
M_k M_{k'}^\hc \right]
\approx
\frac{1}{4}\Re[\Delta_k(s)\Delta_{k'}^\hc(s)
L^{\rho\sigma} N_{\rho\sigma}],
\ee

with ``electron'' ($L^{\rho\sigma}$) and ``chargino'' 
($N_{\rho\sigma}$) tensors given by   
\bem
L^{\rho\sigma} & = & \sum_{\rpa, \rpb}
    [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\rpa}(\ppa)(A' + B'\gd{5})\gu{\sigma}u_{\rpb}(\ppb)]
\nel 
& = &     
{1 \over 4} 
    \Tr\Bigl[(\qcpb + 1)\gu\rho(A - B\gd5)
             (\qcpa - 1)(A' + B'\gd5)\gu\sigma\Bigr] ,
\ee

and
\bem
N_{\rho\sigma} & = & \sum_{\ra, \rb}
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gd{\rho}(a - b\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    [\ub_{\rb}(\pb)(a' + b'\gd{5})\gd{\sigma}v_{\ra}(\pa)]
\nel
& = &     
{1 \over 4} 
\Tr\Bigl[(\qca - 1)\gu\rho(a - b\gd5)(\qcb + 1)(a' + b'\gd5)\gu\sigma\Bigr]
.
\ee

The chargino tensor $N_{\rho\sigma}$ 
can be expressed through $L^{\rho\sigma}(A, A', B, B', \qpa, \qpb)$ via 
\bel{NthroughL}
N_{\rho\sigma} 
   = L_{\rho\sigma}(A\rightarrow a, A'\rightarrow a', 
                   B \rightarrow b, B' \rightarrow b', 
                   \qpa \rightarrow -\qb, \qpb \rightarrow -\qa) 
.
\ee
Thus it is necessary to calculate only one quantity.

\P

Applying properties of $\gamma$-matrices one finds for $L^{\rho\sigma}$
\bem
L^{\rho\sigma} & = &
\frac{1}{4}
    \Tr\Bigl[
        \qcpb\gu\rho(A - B\gd5)\qcpa(A' + B'\gd5)\gu\sigma
        - \gu\rho(A - B\gd5)(A' + B'\gd5)\gu\sigma\Bigr]
\nel & = &
\frac{1}{4}
    \Tr\Bigl[
        \qcpb\gu\rho(A - B\gd5)(A' - B'\gd5)\qcpa\gu\sigma
        - \gu\rho(A - B\gd5)(A' + B'\gd5)\gu\sigma\Bigr]
\nel & = &
\frac{1}{4}
    \Tr\Bigl[
        \qcpb\gu\rho\lbrace 
            AA' + BB' - \gd5(AB' + BA')
        \rbrace \qcpa\gu\sigma
\nel & & \qquad{}
        - \gu\rho\lbrace
            AA' - BB' + \gd5(AB' - BA')
        \rbrace \gu\sigma\Bigr]
\nel & = &
\frac{1}{4}
    \Tr\Bigl[
        \qcpb\gu\rho\lbrace 
            AA' + BB' - \gd5(AB' + BA')
        \rbrace \qcpa\gu\sigma
        - \gu\rho(AA' - BB')\gu\sigma\Bigr]
\nel & = &
\frac{1}{4}\Bigl[
    (AA' + BB')\Tr(\qcpb\gu\rho\qcpa\gu\sigma)
    -(AB' + BA')\Tr(\qcpb\gu\rho\qcpa\gu\sigma\gd5)
\nel & & \qquad {}
    -(AA' - BB')\Tr(\gu\rho\gu\sigma)
    \Bigr]
    .
\ee

I calculate the traces with the help of \rf{gammaTraces}, 

\bem
L^{\rho\sigma} & = &
    (AA' + BB')
        [\qpb^\rho \qpa^\sigma + \qpb^\sigma \qpa^\rho 
        - 
        (\qpb\cdot\qpa)g^{\rho\sigma}]
\nel & & {} + 
    i(AB' + BA')
        \varepsilon^{\mu\rho\nu\sigma}\qpb_\mu \qpa_\nu
    -(AA' - BB')g^{\rho\sigma}
\nel 
 & = & L_S^{\rho\sigma} + L_A^{\rho\sigma},
\ee

where
\bem
L_S^{\rho\sigma}  & = & 
    (AA' + BB')[2\qpb^{(\rho} \qpa^{\sigma)}  
     - (\qpb\cdot\qpa)g^{\rho\sigma}]
    -(AA' - BB')g^{\rho\sigma}
,
\nel

L_A^{\rho\sigma}  & = & 
    i(AB' + BA')
        \varepsilon^{\mu\rho\nu\sigma}\qpb_\mu \qpa_\nu
\ee

In the last expression I explicitly write the symmetric part 
$L_S^{\rho\sigma}=L_S^{(\sigma\rho)}$ and the asymmetric one 
$L_A^{\rho\sigma}=L_A^{[\sigma\rho]}$.

\P
From~\rf{NthroughL} I have for the chargino tensor $N_{\rho\sigma}$,
\bem
N_{\rho\sigma} & = & {N_S}_{\rho\sigma} + {N_A}_{\rho\sigma},
\nel
{N_S}_{\rho\sigma} & =  & 
    (aa' + bb')[2\qa_{(\rho} \qb_{\sigma)} 
     - (\qb\cdot\qa)g_{\rho\sigma}]
    -(aa' - bb')g_{\rho\sigma}
,
\nel 
{N_A}_{\rho\sigma} & =  & 
    i(ab' + ba')
        \varepsilon_{\eta\rho\theta\sigma}\qa^\eta \qb^\theta
        .
\ee

Two products will be required. I first consider the symmetric parts:

\bem
L_S^{\rho\sigma} {N_S}_{\rho\sigma}
& =  &
\{
    (AA' + BB')[2\qpb^{(\rho} \qpa^{\sigma)}  
     - (\qpb\cdot\qpa)g^{\rho\sigma}]
    -(AA' - BB')g^{\rho\sigma}
\}
\nel && {} \times
\{
    (aa' + bb')[2\qa_{(\rho} \qb_{\sigma)} 
     - (\qb\cdot\qa)g_{\rho\sigma}]
    -(aa' - bb')g_{\rho\sigma}
\}

\nel
& = & 
(aa' + bb')(AA' + BB')
\nel && \qquad {} \times 
[2\qpb^{(\rho} \qpa^{\sigma)} - (\qpb\cdot\qpa)g^{\rho\sigma}]
[2\qa_{\rho} \qb_{\sigma} - (\qb\cdot\qa)g_{\rho\sigma}]
\nel 
&& {}
- (aa' - bb')(AA' + BB')[2(\qpb \cdot \qpa) - 4(\qpb\cdot\qpa)]
\nel && {}
- (aa' + bb')(AA' - BB')[2(\qb \cdot \qa) - 4(\qb\cdot\qa)]
\nel 
&& {}
+ 4(aa' - bb')(AA' - BB')
\nel
& = & 
(aa' + bb')(AA' + BB')
[2(\qpb \cdot \qa) (\qpa \cdot \qb) + 2(\qpb \cdot \qb) (\qpa \cdot \qa)
\nel
&& \qquad {}
- 2 (\qb\cdot\qa) (\qpb \cdot \qpa) - 2 (\qb\cdot\qa) (\qpb \cdot \qpa)
 + 4 (\qb\cdot\qa) (\qpb \cdot \qpa)]
\nel
&& {}
+ 2(aa' - bb')(AA' + BB')(\qpb\cdot\qpa)
\nel
&& {}
+ 2(aa' + bb')(AA' - BB')(\qb\cdot\qa)
+ 4(aa' - bb')(AA' - BB')
\nel
& = & 
2(aa' + bb')(AA' + BB')
[(\qpb \cdot \qa) (\qpa \cdot \qb) + (\qpb \cdot \qb) (\qpa \cdot \qa)]
\nel
&& {}
+ 2(aa' - bb')(AA' + BB')(\qpb\cdot\qpa)
\nel
&& {}
+ 2(aa' + bb')(AA' - BB')(\qb\cdot\qa)
+ 4(aa' - bb')(AA' - BB')
.
\ee

To calculate $L_A^{\rho\sigma} {N_A}_{\rho\sigma}$ I use the identity
\be
\varepsilon^{\mu\rho\nu\sigma} \varepsilon_{\eta\rho\theta\sigma}
= -2
(\delta^\mu_\eta \delta^\nu_\theta - \delta^\mu_\theta \delta^\nu_\eta)
.
\ee

Thus
\bem
L_A^{\rho\sigma} {N_A}_{\rho\sigma}
& = & 
-(ab' + ba')(AB' + BA')
        \varepsilon^{\mu\rho\nu\sigma}\qpb_\mu \qpa_\nu
        \varepsilon_{\eta\rho\theta\sigma}\qa^\eta \qb^\theta
\nel
& = & 
2(ab' + ba')(AB' + BA')
(\delta^\mu_\eta \delta^\nu_\theta - \delta^\mu_\theta \delta^\nu_\eta)
\qpb_\mu \qpa_\nu
\qa^\eta \qb^\theta

\nel
& = & 
2(ab' + ba')(AB' + BA')
[(\qpb \cdot \qa) (\qpa \cdot \qb) - (\qpb \cdot \qb) (\qpa \cdot \qa)]
.
\ee

Now I use~\rf{contractionWithSymAnti} to simplify the product 
$L^{\rho\sigma} N_{\rho\sigma}$,
\beml{chargeLMIs}
\lefteqn{
L^{\rho\sigma} N_{\rho\sigma}
= 
(L_S^{\rho\sigma} + L_A^{\rho\sigma})({N_S}_{\rho\sigma} + {N_A}_{\rho\sigma})
= L_S^{\rho\sigma} {N_S}_{\rho\sigma} + L_A^{\rho\sigma} {N_A}_{\rho\sigma}
}
\nel
& = & 
2(aa' + bb')(AA' + BB')
[(\qpb \cdot \qa) (\qpa \cdot \qb) + (\qpb \cdot \qb) (\qpa \cdot \qa)]
\nel
&& {} 
+ 2(ab' + ba')(AB' + BA')
[(\qpb \cdot \qa) (\qpa \cdot \qb) - (\qpb \cdot \qb) (\qpa \cdot \qa)]
\nel
&& {}
+ 2(aa' - bb')(AA' + BB')(\qpb\cdot\qpa)
\nel
&& {}
+ 2(aa' + bb')(AA' - BB')(\qb\cdot\qa)
\nel
&& {}
+ 4(aa' - bb')(AA' - BB')
.
\ee

\P
Under the approximations $m_\chi \gg m_e$, $s \gg 4m_e$
I may use the formula~\rf{cs-q-scalar-products}
where $m = m_e$, $m' = m_\chi$,
to calculate the kinematic quantities in \rf{chargeLMIs}, 
\bem
(\qpb \cdot \qa)(\qpa \cdot \qb) + (\qpb \cdot \qb) (\qpa \cdot \qa)
&\approx& {1 \over 4m_e^2 m_\chi^2}[2(m_\chi^2 - t)^2 + s(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2)],
\nel
(\qpb \cdot \qa)(\qpa \cdot \qb) - (\qpb \cdot \qb) (\qpa \cdot \qa)
&\approx& {s \over 4 m_e^2 m_\chi^2}(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2),
\nel
(\qb\cdot\qa) &\approx& {s \over 2 m_e^2}
,
\ee

\be
t = (\ppa - \pa)^2 = (\pb - \ppb)^2
.
\ee

I also may neglect $2(aa' - bb')(AA' + BB')(\qpb\cdot\qpa)$ and
$4(aa' - bb')(AA' - BB')$ under 
the same approximation because they do not contain $m_e^{-2}$. Thus
\bem
\lefteqn{
L^{\rho\sigma} N_{\rho\sigma}
}
\nel
& \approx  & 
{1 \over 2m_e^2 m_\chi^2} \bigl\{
(aa' + bb')(AA' + BB')[2(m_\chi^2 - t)^2 + s(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2)]
\nel && \qquad {}
+ (ab' + ba')(AB' + BA')s(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2) \bigr\}
\nel
&& {}
+ {s \over m_e^2}(aa' + bb')(AA' - BB')

\nel
& = & 
{(m_\chi^2 - t)^2 \over m_e^2 m_\chi^2} (aa' + bb')(AA' + BB')
\nel && {}
+
{s(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2) \over 2m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
[(ab' + ba')(AB' + BA') + (aa' + bb')(AA' + BB')]
\nel
&& {}
+ {s \over m_e^2}(aa' + bb')(AA' - BB')
,
\ee

which is a real quantity so $X_{kk'}$ of the equation~\rf{charge-X-boson-1}
is given by

\beml{charge-X1}
\lefteqn{
X_{kk'} \approx \Re[\Delta_k(s)\Delta_{k'}^\hc(s)]
}
\nel
& \times & \Bigl\{
{(m_\chi^2 - t)^2 \over 4m_e^2 m_\chi^2} 
(a_{k}a_{k'} + b_{k}b_{k'})(A_{k}A_{k'} + B_{k}B_{k'})
\nel
&& {}
+
{s(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2) \over 8m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
\nel
&& \quad{}\times
[(a_{k}b_{k'} + b_{k}a_{k'})(A_{k}B_{k'} + B_{k}A_{k'}) 
    + (a_{k}a_{k'} + b_{k}b_{k'})(A_{k'}A_{k'} + B_{k}B_{k'})]
\nel
&& {}
+ {s \over 4m_e^2}(a_{k}a_{k'} + b_{k}b_{k'})(A_{k}A_{k'} - B_{k}B_{k'})
\Bigr\}
.
\ee


\subsection{Contribution from the scalar neutrino channel}


The calculation of $X_{\tilde\nu\tilde\nu}$ 
(see~\rf{charge-sneutrino-X-definition}) 
follows the same pattern as for $X_{kk'}$,


\be
X_{\tilde\nu\tilde\nu} = 
\frac{1}{4}\Re\left[\sum_{\ra, \rb, \rpa, \rpb}
M_{\tilde\nu} M_{\tilde\nu}^{\hc}\right]
=
\frac{1}{4}\abs{\Delta_{\tilde\nu}(t)}^2\Re[L N],
\ee

where $L$ and $N$ come from~\rf{chargeMTriple} and~\rf{chargeMShortExp}, 
\bem
L & = & \sum_{\rpa, \rb}
    [\ub_{\rpa}(\ppa)(F + G\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    [\ub_{\rb}(\pb)(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})u_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
\nel
& = & 
\frac{1}{4} \Tr
\Bigl[(\qcpa + 1)(F + G\gd{5})
      (\qcb + 1)(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})\Bigr]
,
\ee

and
\beml{charge-N-tmp23}
N & = &  \sum_{\ra, \rpb}  
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})v_{\rpb}(\ppb)]
    [\vb_{\rpb}(\ppb)(F + G\gd{5})v_{\ra}(\pa)]
\nel
& = & 
\frac{1}{4}\Tr
\Bigl[(\qca - 1)(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})
      (\qcpb - 1)(F + G\gd{5})\Bigr]
\nel & = &
L(\qpa \rightarrow -\qpb, \qb \rightarrow -\qa).
\ee

Now I simplify $L$:
\bem
L 
& = &
\frac{1}{4} \Tr
\Bigl[\qcpa(F + G\gd{5})\qcb(\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})
      + (F + G\gd{5})(\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})
\Bigr]

\nel & = &
\frac{1}{4} \Tr
\Bigl[\qcpa\qcb(F - G\gd{5})(\cc{F} - \cc{G}\gd{5})
      + \abs{F}^2 - \abs{G}^2
\Bigr]

\nel & = &
\frac{1}{4} \Tr
\Bigl[\qcpa\qcb(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2)
      + \abs{F}^2 - \abs{G}^2
\Bigr]

\nel & = &
(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2)(\qpa \cdot \qb) + \abs{F}^2 - \abs{G}^2.
\ee

The relation~\rf{charge-N-tmp23} gives for $N$
\bem
N 
& = &
(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2)(\qpb \cdot \qa) + \abs{F}^2 - \abs{G}^2
\; = \; L 
.
\ee

Thus I have
\bem
X_{\tilde\nu\tilde\nu} & = & 

\frac{1}{4}\abs{\Delta_{\tilde\nu}(t)}^2\Re\left\{
[(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2)(\qpa \cdot \qb) + \abs{F}^2 - \abs{G}^2]^2
\right\}

\nel  
& = & 
\frac{1}{4}\abs{\Delta_{\tilde\nu}(t)}^2
[(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2)(\qpa \cdot \qb) + \abs{F}^2 - \abs{G}^2]^2

\nel & \approx & 
\frac{1}{4}\abs{\Delta_{\tilde\nu}(t)}^2
(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2)^2(\qpa \cdot \qb)^2 
,
\ee

or

\bel{charge-X2}
X_{\tilde\nu\tilde\nu} \approx 
\abs{\Delta_{\tilde\nu}(t)}^2{(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2 \over 16 m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2)^2
,
\ee

where I used \rf{csKinApproximation} in the last step.

\subsection{Interference contribution.}


To find $X_{k\tilde\nu}$ I write for it according to~\rf{chargeMShortExp}, 
\be
X_{k\tilde\nu} \approx
\frac{1}{4}\Re\left[\sum_{\ra, \rb, \rpa, \rpb}
M_k M_{\tilde\nu}^{\hc}\right]
=
\frac{1}{4}\Re[\Delta_k(s)\Delta_{\tilde\nu}^\hc(t) K]
,
\ee

with $K$ given by
\bem
K  
& = & \sum_{\ra, \rb, \rpa, \rpb}
    [\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gd{\rho}(a - b\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
\nel && \qquad
{} \times 
    [\ub_{\rb}(\pb)(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})u_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\rpb}(\ppb)(F + G\gd{5})v_{\ra}(\pa)]
.
\ee

I transform $K$ to the form necessary to calculate the spin
polarization sums with the 
help of~\rf{gammaCProperties} and~\rf{gammaSpinorRelation}, 

\beml{charge-before-spin-sums-1}
[\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
& = &
[\ub_{\rpb}(\ppb)\gu{\rho}(A - B\gd{5})v_{\rpa}(\ppa)]^T
\nel & = &
[v_{\rpa}^T(\ppa)(A - B\gd{5}^T)\gu{\rho}^T\ub_{\rpb}^T(\ppb)]
\nel & = &
[v_{\rpa}^T(\ppa)CC^{-1}(A - B\gd{5}^T)\gu{\rho}^TCC^{-1}\ub_{\rpb}^T(\ppb)]
\nel & = &
-[\ub_{\rpa}(\ppa)(A - B C^{-1}\gd{5}^T C)C^{-1}\gu{\rho}^TC v_{\rpb}(\ppb)]
\nel & = &
[\ub_{\rpa}(\ppa)(A - B \gd{5})\gu{\rho} v_{\rpb}(\ppb)]
.
\ee
    
Now I can represent $K$ as a trace
\bem
K  
& = & \sum_{\ra, \rb, \rpa, \rpb}
    [\ub_{\rpa}(\ppa)(A - B \gd{5})\gu{\rho} v_{\rpb}(\ppb)]
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gd{\rho}(a - b\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
\nel && \qquad
{} \times 
    [\ub_{\rb}(\pb)(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})u_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
    [\vb_{\rpb}(\ppb)(F + G\gd{5})v_{\ra}(\pa)]
\nel
& = & \sum_{\ra, \rb, \rpa, \rpb}
    [\ub_{\rpa}(\ppa)(A - B \gd{5})\gu{\rho} v_{\rpb}(\ppb)]
    [\vb_{\rpb}(\ppb)(F + G\gd{5})v_{\ra}(\pa)]
\nel && \qquad
{} \times 
    [\vb_{\ra}(\pa)\gd{\rho}(a - b\gd{5})u_{\rb}(\pb)]
    [\ub_{\rb}(\pb)(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})u_{\rpa}(\ppa)]
\nel
& = & {1 \over 16} \Tr \Bigl[
    (\qcpa + 1)(A - B \gd{5})\gu{\rho} (\qcpb - 1)(F + G\gd{5})
\nel && \qquad
{} \times 
    (\qca - 1) \gd{\rho}(a - b\gd{5}) (\qcb + 1)(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})
    \Bigr]
.
\ee

\P

To simplify $K$ I use $m_\chi \gg m_e$, thus I can omit terms without 
$m_e^{-2}$ that come from $\qca = \pca/m_e$, $\qcb = \pcb/m_e$. I also 
keep only terms with an even number of Dirac matrices, 
\bem
K  
& \approx & 
{1 \over 16} \Tr [
    \qcpa(A - B \gd{5})\gu{\rho}\qcpb(F + G\gd{5})
    \qca \gd{\rho}(a - b\gd{5}) \qcb(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})
    ]
\nel && {} 
-
{1 \over 16} \Tr [
    (A - B \gd{5})\gu{\rho}(F + G\gd{5})
    \qca \gd{\rho}(a - b\gd{5}) \qcb(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})
    ]
\nel& = &
{1 \over 16} (K_1 - K_2),
\ee

where $K_1$ and $K_2$ are given by
\bem
K_1 & = & 
\Tr [\qcpa(A - B \gd{5})\gu{\rho}\qcpb(F + G\gd{5})
    \qca \gd{\rho}(a - b\gd{5}) \qcb(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})]
\nel & = & 
\Tr [\qcpa\gu{\rho}\qcpb(A - B \gd{5})(F + G\gd{5})
    \qca \gd{\rho}\qcb (a + b\gd{5}) (F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})]
\nel & = & 
\Tr [\qcpa\gu{\rho}\qcpb\qca \gd{\rho}\qcb
     (A + B \gd{5})(F - G\gd{5})
     (a + b\gd{5}) (F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})]
,
\ee

\bem
K_2 & = &
\Tr [(A - B \gd{5})\gu{\rho}(F + G\gd{5})
    \qca \gd{\rho}(a - b\gd{5}) \qcb(F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})]
\nel & = &
\Tr [\gu{\rho}(A + B \gd{5})(F + G\gd{5})
    \qca \gd{\rho}\qcb(a + b\gd{5}) (F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})]
\nel & = &
\Tr [\gu{\rho}\qca \gd{\rho}\qcb(A - B \gd{5})(F - G\gd{5})
    (a + b\gd{5}) (F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})]
.
\ee

To find the sandwich-like products $\gu{\rho} \dots \gd{\rho}$ I use 
the basic properties \rf{gammaAlgebra} of the Dirac algebra,

\be
\gu{\rho}\qca \gd{\rho} = -\gu{\rho}\gd{\rho}\qca + 2\gu{\rho} \qa_\rho
= -4\qca + 2\qca = - 2\qca, 
\ee

and
\be
\gu{\rho}\qcpb\qca \gd{\rho}
= 
-\gu{\rho}\qcpb\gd{\rho}\qca + 2 \gu{\rho}\qcpb \qa_\rho 
= 2\qcpb \qca + 2 \qca \qcpb = 2\{\qcpb, \qca\} = 4 (\qpb \cdot \qa)
.
\ee

It gives for $K_1$ and $K_2$ 

\bem
K_1 & = & 4 (\qpb \cdot \qa) \Tr [\qcpa\qcb
     (A + B \gd{5})(F - G\gd{5})
     (a + b\gd{5}) (F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})]
\nel & = &
4 (\qpb \cdot \qa) \Tr [\qcpa\qcb
\nel && \qquad {} \times
     \{AF - BG +\gd5(BF - AG)\}
     \{aF^\hc - bG^\hc +\gd5(bF^\hc - aG^\hc)\}]
\nel & = &
4 (\qpb \cdot \qa) \Tr [\qcpa\qcb
     \{(AF - BG)(aF^\hc - bG^\hc) + (BF - AG)(bF^\hc - aG^\hc) \} ]

\nel & = &
16 (\qpb \cdot \qa)(\qpa \cdot \qb)
[(AF - BG)(aF^\hc - bG^\hc) + (BF - AG)(bF^\hc - aG^\hc)]
\nel & = &
16 (\qpb \cdot \qa)(\qpa \cdot \qb)
[(aA + bB)\abs{F}^2  + (aA + bB)\abs{G}^2
\nel & & \quad {}
- GF^\hc(bA + aB) - FG^\hc(bA + aB)]
\nel & = &
16 (\qpb \cdot \qa)(\qpa \cdot \qb)
[(aA + bB)(\abs{F}^2  + \abs{G}^2) - 2\Re(FG^\hc)(bA + aB)]
,
\ee

and
\bem
K_2 & = &
-2 \Tr [\qca\qcb(A - B \gd{5})(F - G\gd{5})
    (a + b\gd{5}) (F^\hc - G^\hc\gd{5})]
\nel & = &
-2 \Tr [\qca\qcb
     \{AF + BG - \gd5(BF + AG)\}
     \{aF^\hc - bG^\hc +\gd5(bF^\hc - aG^\hc)\}]
\nel & = &
-2 \Tr [\qca\qcb
     \{(AF + BG)(aF^\hc - bG^\hc) + (BF + AG)(aG^\hc - bF^\hc)\}]
\nel & = &
-8(\qb \cdot \qa) 
[(AF + BG)(aF^\hc - bG^\hc) + (BF + AG)(aG^\hc - bF^\hc)]
\nel & = &
-8(\qb \cdot \qa) 
[(aA -bB)\abs{F}^2 + (aA - bB)\abs{G}^2 
\nel & & \quad {}
+ GF^\hc(aB - bA) + FG^\hc(aB - bA)]
\nel & = &
-8(\qb \cdot \qa) 
[(aA - bB)(\abs{F}^2  + \abs{G}^2) + 2\Re(FG^\hc)(aB - bA)]
,
\ee

where I used~\rf{gammaTraces} and~\rf{gammaZeroTraces} 
together with $\gd5^2 = 1$. Thus

\bem
K & \approx & 
{1 \over 16} (K_1 - K_2)
\nel
& = & 
(\qpb \cdot \qa)(\qpa \cdot \qb)
[(aA + bB)(\abs{F}^2  + \abs{G}^2) - 2\Re(FG^\hc)(bA + aB)]
\nel && {} + 
{1 \over 2} (\qb \cdot \qa) 
[(aA - bB)(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2) - 2\Re(FG^\hc)(bA - aB)]
\nel & \approx & 
{(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2 \over 4 m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
[(aA + bB)(\abs{F}^2  + \abs{G}^2) - 2\Re(FG^\hc)(bA + aB)]
\nel && {} + 
{s \over 4m_e^2}
[(aA - bB)(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2) - 2\Re(FG^\hc)(bA - aB)]
\ee

from~\rf{csKinApproximation}. 

\P
Now I have for the interference contribution $X_{k\tilde\nu}$ 

\beml{charge-X3}
X_{k\tilde\nu} & \approx & 
\frac{1}{4}\Re[\Delta_k(s)\Delta_{\tilde\nu}^\hc(t) K]
\nel & \approx & 
{(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2 \over 16 m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
\Re[\Delta_k(s) \Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}^\hc(t)]
\nel && \qquad {} \times
[(a_k A_k + b_kB_k)(\abs{F}^2  + \abs{G}^2) - 2\Re(FG^\hc)(b_k A_k + a_k B_k)]
\nel && {} + 
{s \over 16 m_e^2}
\Re[\Delta_k(s) \Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}^\hc(t)] 
\nel && \qquad {} \times
[(a_k A_k - b_k B_k)(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2) - 2\Re(FG^\hc)(b_k A_k - a_k B_k)]
.
\ee

\subsection{Differential cross section}

According to~\rf{chargeFullX} and~\rf{csDifApproximation} 
the differential cross section for the chargino production process
is given by

\be
\left({d\sigma \over d\Omega }\right)_{\rm c.m.}
\approx {m_e^2 m_\chi^2  \over 4\pi^2 s} \sqrt{1 - 4\rho}X_{e\chi}(s, t),
\ee

where
\be
\rho = {m_\chi^2 \over s},
\ee

and
\bel{charge-differential-as-a-sum}
X_{e\chi}(s, t)
= X_{\gamma\gamma} + X_{ZZ} + 2X_{\gamma Z} + X_{\tilde\nu\tilde\nu} 
+ 2X_{\gamma\tilde\nu} + 2X_{Z\tilde\nu}
.
\ee

The first three terms in~\rf{charge-differential-as-a-sum} are given 
by~\rf{charge-X1},

\beml{charge-d-gamma-gamma}
X_{\gamma\gamma} & \approx & { \Delta_\gamma^2(s) \over 4m_e^2 m_\chi^2 }
\Bigl\{
(m_\chi^2 - t)^2 a_\gamma^2 A_\gamma^2
+ {1 \over 2} s(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2) a_\gamma^2 A_\gamma^2
+ s m_\chi^2 a_\gamma^2 A_\gamma^2 \Bigr\}
\nel
& = &
{e^4 \over 4s^2m_e^2 m_\chi^2 }
[(m_\chi^2 - t)^2  + s m_\chi^2
+ {1 \over 2} s(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2)
],
\ee

where I use~\rf{chargeGabIs}, \rf{chargeGABIs} and~\rf{photonPropogator},
\be
-a_\gamma = A_\gamma = e, \quad b_\gamma = B_\gamma = 0,
\quad \Delta_\gamma(s) = {1 \over s}.
\ee

The explicit form of $X_{ZZ}$ is
\bem
X_{ZZ} & \approx & {\abs{\Delta_Z(s)}^2 \over  4m_e^2 m_\chi^2 }
\Bigl\{
(m_\chi^2 - t)^2 (a_Z^2 + b_Z^2)(A_Z^2 + B_Z^2)
\nel
&& {}
+
{1 \over 2} s(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2) 
[(2a_Z b_Z)(2A_Z B_Z) + (a_Z^2 + b_Z^2)(A_Z^2 + B_Z^2)]
\nel
&& {}
+ sm_\chi^2(a_Z^2 + b_Z^2)(A_Z^2 - B_Z^2)
\Bigr\}.
\ee

I find for the coefficients in the last expressions

\bem
a_Z^2 + b_Z^2 & = &
{g^2 \over 16\cos^2\theta_W}[(1 - 4\sin^2\theta_W)^2 + 1]
\nel &=& {g^2 \over 8\cos^2\theta_W}(1 - 4\sin^2\theta_W + 8\sin^4\theta_W)
\quad (\mbox{from \rf{chargeZabIs}})
,
\ee

and
\be
a_Z b_Z = 
{g^2 \over 16\cos^2\theta_W}(1 - 4\sin^2\theta_W)
.
\ee

The definitions~\rf{chargeZABIs} lead to
\bem
A_Z^2 \pm B_Z^2 &= &
{g^2 \over 16\cos^2\theta_W}
\{
[2\cos (2\theta_W) + \abs{Y_{11}}^2 + \abs{X_{11}}^2]^2
\pm [\abs{Y_{11}}^2 - \abs{X_{11}}^2]^2
\}
,
\nel
A_Z B_Z &= &
-{g^2 \over 16\cos^2\theta_W}
[2\cos (2\theta_W) + \abs{Y_{11}}^2 + \abs{X_{11}}^2]
[\abs{Y_{11}}^2 - \abs{X_{11}}^2]
.
\ee

Thus
\beml{charge-d-Z-Z}
X_{ZZ} & \approx & {a_Z^2 + b_Z^2 \over 4m_e^2 m_\chi^2}\abs{\Delta_Z(s)}^2
[
(m_\chi^2 - t)^2 (A_Z^2 + B_Z^2) + s m_\chi^2 (A_Z^2 - B_Z^2)
]
\nel &&{} +
{s(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2) \over 8m_e^2 m_\chi^2} \abs{\Delta_Z(s)}^2
[4 a_Z b_Z A_Z B_Z + (a_Z^2 + b_Z^2)(A_Z^2 + B_Z^2)] .
\nel
\ee

\P
The interference between the $\gamma$ and $Z^0$ channels reads,
according to the equation~\rf{charge-X1}
\beml{charge-d-gamma-Z}
\lefteqn{
X_{\gamma Z}
}
\nel & \approx & {\Delta_\gamma(s)\Re(\Delta_Z^\hc(s)) \over 4m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
\Bigl\{
(m_\chi^2 - t)^2(a_\gamma a_Z)(A_\gamma A_Z)
\nel
&& {}
+
{1\over 2} s(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2)
[(a_\gamma b_Z)(A_\gamma B_Z) + (a_\gamma a_Z)(A_\gamma A_Z)]
\nel
&& {}
+ s m_\chi^2(a_\gamma a_Z)(A_\gamma A_Z)
\Bigr\}
\nel
& = &
-{e^2 a_Z A_Z \over 4sm_e^2 m_\chi^2} \Re(\Delta_Z(s))
\nel&& {} \quad \times
\Biggl[
(m_\chi^2 - t)^2 
+
{1 \over 2}s(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2) 
\left( {b_Z B_Z\over a_Z A_Z} + 1\right)
+ sm_\chi^2
\Biggr]
.
%\nel
\ee

\P
The expression for $X_{\tilde\nu\tilde\nu}$ is given by~\rf{charge-X2}.
If I substitute there the definitions~\rf{chargeFandG1} and~\rf{chargeFandG2},
then
\be
\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2 \approx {g^2 \over 2}\cos^2\theta_X, 
\quad
2\Re(FG^\hc) \approx {g^2 \over 2}\cos^2\theta_X,
\ee

where according to~\rf{lagrangian-theta-X-and-Y} 
and~\rf{lagrangian-eta-X-and-Y}
\be
\cos^2\theta_X = {1 \over 2 + 2 \eta_X^2 - 2\sqrt{1 + \eta_X^2}}, 
\quad 
\eta_X = 
   \frac{m_{\tilde\Lambda}^2 - \abs{\mu}^2 + 2m_W^2\cos(2\beta)}
        {2\sqrt{2}m_W\abs{m_{\tilde\Lambda}\sin\beta + \mu^* \cos\beta}}
        .
\ee


Thus
\beml{charge-d-nu-nu}
X_{\tilde\nu\tilde\nu} &\approx &
\abs{\Delta_{\tilde\nu}(t)}^2{(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2 \over 16 m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2)^2
\nel
& = &
g^4 \cos^4\theta_X
\abs{\Delta_{\tilde\nu}(t)}^2{(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2 \over 64 m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
.
\ee

Now I use~\rf{charge-X3} to write the photon--$\tilde\nu$ 
interference term $X_{\gamma\tilde\nu}$

\beml{charge-d-gamma-nu}
X_{\gamma\tilde\nu} & \approx & 
{(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2 \over 16 m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
\Delta_\gamma(s) \Re[\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}^\hc(t)]
\nel && \qquad {} \times
[(a_\gamma A_\gamma)(\abs{F}^2  + \abs{G}^2)]
\nel && {} + 
{s \over 16 m_e^2}
\Delta_\gamma(s)\Re[\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}^\hc(t)] 
[(a_\gamma A_\gamma)(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2)]

\nel & = &
-{e^2 (\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2) \over 16s m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
\Re[\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(t)]
\left[(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2 + s m_\chi^2\right]

\nel & = &
-{e^2 g^2 \cos^2\theta_X \over 32 s m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
\Re[\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(t)]
\left[(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2 + s^2\rho \right]
.
\ee

In a similar way $X_{Z\tilde\nu}$ becomes
\beml{charge-d-gamma-nu1}
X_{Z\tilde\nu} & \approx & 
{(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2 \over 16 m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
\Re[\Delta_Z(s) \Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}^\hc(t)]
\nel && \qquad {} \times
[(a_Z A_Z + b_Z B_Z)(\abs{F}^2  + \abs{G}^2) - 2\Re(FG^\hc)(b_Z A_Z + a_Z B_Z)]
\nel && {} + 
{s \over 16 m_e^2}
\Re[\Delta_Z(s) \Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}^\hc(t)] 
\nel && \qquad {} \times
[(a_Z A_Z - b_Z B_Z)(\abs{F}^2 + \abs{G}^2) - 2\Re(FG^\hc)(b_Z A_Z - a_Z B_Z)]
\nel
& = &
{1 \over 16 m_e^2 m_\chi^2 } \Re[\Delta_Z(s) \Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}^\hc(t)]
\nel && {}\times
{g^2 \cos^2\theta_X \over 2}
\Bigl[
(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2(a_Z A_Z + b_Z B_Z - b_Z A_Z - a_Z B_Z)
\nel && {}\qquad
+
sm_\chi^2(a_Z A_Z - b_Z B_Z - b_Z A_Z + a_Z B_Z)
\Bigr]
\nel
& = &
{a_Z - b_Z \over 16 m_e^2 m_\chi^2 } \Re[\Delta_Z(s) \Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}^\hc(t)]
\nel && {}\times
{g^2 \cos^2\theta_X \over 2}
\Bigl[
(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2(A_Z - B_Z)
+
sm_\chi^2(A_Z + B_Z)
\Bigr]
.
\ee

From~\rf{chargeZabIs} and~\rf{chargeZABIs} I have
\be
a_Z - b_Z  =  {g \sin^2 \theta_W \over \cos \theta_W},
\ee

and
\bem
A_Z + B_Z &= &
{g \over 4\cos\theta_W}
\{[2\cos (2\theta_W) + \abs{Y_{11}}^2 + \abs{X_{11}}^2] 
- [\abs{Y_{11}}^2 - \abs{X_{11}}^2]\}
\nel
& = &
{g \over 2\cos\theta_W}[\cos(2\theta_W) + \abs{X_{11}}^2]
= 
{g \over 2\cos\theta_W}[\cos(2\theta_W) + \cos^2\theta_X]
,
\nel
A_Z - B_Z & = &
{g \over 2\cos\theta_W}[\cos(2\theta_W) + \abs{Y_{11}}^2]
= 
{g \over 2\cos\theta_W}[\cos(2\theta_W) + \cos^2\theta_Y],
\ee

Thus
\beml{charge-d-Z-nu}
X_{Z\tilde\nu} & \approx & 
{g^4 \tan^2 \theta_W \cos^2\theta_X \over 64 m_e^2 m_\chi^2}
 \Re[\Delta_Z(s) \Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}^\hc(t)]
\nel && {} \times
\Bigl[
(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2(\cos(2\theta_W) + \cos^2\theta_Y)
+
sm_\chi^2(\cos(2\theta_W) + \cos^2\theta_X)
\Bigr]
\nel
.
\ee


\subsection{Total cross section}

I have for the total cross-section from~\rf{csTotalApproximation},
\bel{chargeCross}
\sigma^T_{e\chi}(s)  
= {m_e^2 m_\chi^2 \over \pi s^2} \int_{t_-}^{t_+} 
X_{e\chi}(s, t) \, dt
= \sigma^T_{\gamma\gamma} 
+ \sigma^T_{ZZ} 
+ 2\sigma^T_{\gamma Z} 
+ \sigma^T_{\tilde{\nu}\tilde{\nu}} 
+ 2\sigma^T_{\gamma \tilde{\nu}} 
+ 2\sigma^T_{Z \tilde{\nu}}
,
\ee

with the limits of integration
\bel{charge-t-plus-minus-is}
t_{\pm} \approx {s \over 2} (2\rho - 1 \pm \sqrt{1 - 4\rho})
.
\ee

I use \rf{csInt1}--\rf{csInt65} to calculate the integrals  
in~\rf{chargeCross} together with the 
expressions~\rf{chargeSneutrinoDelta} and~\rf{ZPropogator} for 
$\Delta_Z(s)$ and $\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(t)$,
\be
\Delta_Z(s) 
= {1 \over s - \mB^2 + i\mB\Gamma_Z} 
= {s - \mB^2 - i\mB\Gamma_Z \over (s - \mB^2)^2 + \mB^2\Gamma_Z^2},
\ee

\bel{charge-x-is}
\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(t) = {1 \over t - x} ,
\quad x =  m_{\tilde{\nu}}^2 - im_{\tilde{\nu}}\Gamma_{\tilde{\nu}}
.
\ee

I obtain for the photon-channel contribution from~\rf{charge-d-gamma-gamma}  

\bem
\sigma^T_{\gamma\gamma} & = & 
{m_e^2 m_\chi^2 \over \pi s^2} \int_{t_-}^{t_+} X_{\gamma\gamma} dt
\nel
&\approx&
{e^4 \over 4\pi s^4 }
\int_{t_-}^{t_+}[(m_\chi^2 - t)^2  + s m_\chi^2 
+ {1 \over 2} s(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2)]dt
\nel&=&
 {e^4 \over 4\pi s^4 }
[{s^3 \over 3} (1 - \rho)\sqrt{1 - 4\rho} + s^2 \rho(t_+ - t_-) + 0]
\nel&=&
{e^4 \over 4\pi s^4 }
[{s^3 \over 3} (1 - \rho)\sqrt{1 - 4\rho} + s^3 \rho\sqrt{1 - 4\rho}]
,
\ee

where the integration was carried out with the help 
of~\rf{csInt2} and~\rf{csInt1}. So I find

\bel{charge-total-gamma-gamma}
\sigma^T_{\gamma\gamma}(s) = 
{e^4 \over 4\pi s} \sqrt{1 - 4\rho}{1 + 2\rho \over 3}
.
\ee

Similarly for the $Z^0$-channel contribution, the equation~\rf{charge-d-Z-Z}
yields
\bem
\sigma^T_{ZZ} & = & 
{m_e^2 m_\chi^2 \over \pi s^2} \int_{t_-}^{t_+} X_{ZZ} dt
\nel
& \approx & {a_Z^2 + b_Z^2 \over 4\pi s^2}\abs{\Delta_Z(s)}^2
\int_{t_-}^{t_+}[
(m_\chi^2 - t)^2 (A_Z^2 + B_Z^2) + s m_\chi^2 (A_Z^2 - B_Z^2)
]dt + 0
,
\ee

where again the term in $X_{ZZ}$ proportional to $(s + 2t - 2m_\chi^2)$
does not contribute to the integral. Thus,
\bem
\sigma^T_{ZZ} & = & 
{a_Z^2 + b_Z^2 \over 4\pi s^2}\abs{\Delta_Z(s)}^2
\biggl[{s^3 \over 3} (1 - \rho)\sqrt{1 - 4\rho}(A_Z^2 + B_Z^2)
\nel&& {} \qquad
  + s^3\rho \sqrt{1 - 4\rho}(A_Z^2 - B_Z^2)
\biggr]
\nel
& = & 
{a_Z^2 + b_Z^2 \over 4\pi} s\abs{\Delta_Z(s)}^2
\left[{1 \over 3} (1 - \rho)(A_Z^2 + B_Z^2)
  + \rho (A_Z^2 - B_Z^2)
\right]\sqrt{1 - 4\rho},
\ee

so
\bel{charge-total-Z-Z}
\sigma^T_{ZZ}(s) = 
{a_Z^2 + b_Z^2 \over 4\pi} s\abs{\Delta_Z(s)}^2\sqrt{1 - 4\rho}
\left[A_Z^2{1 + 2\rho \over 3} + B_Z^2{1 - 4\rho \over 3}\right].
\ee

The calculation of $\sigma^T_{\gamma Z}$ is also straightforward. 
I obtain from~\rf{charge-d-gamma-Z}
\bem
\sigma^T_{\gamma Z}(s) & = &
{m_e^2 m_\chi^2 \over \pi s^2} \int_{t_-}^{t_+} X_{Z\gamma} dt
\nel
&\approx&
-{e^2 a_Z A_Z \over 4\pi s^3} \Re(\Delta_Z(s))
\int_{t_-}^{t_+}[(m_\chi^2 - t)^2 + sm_\chi^2]dt 
\nel&=&
-{e^2 a_Z A_Z \over 4\pi s^3} \Re(\Delta_Z(s))
\left[{s^3 \over 3} (1 - \rho)\sqrt{1 - 4\rho} 
   + s^3 \rho\sqrt{1 - 4\rho}\right],
\ee

or
\bel{charge-total-gamma-Z}
\sigma^T_{\gamma Z}(s)
=
-{e^2 a_Z A_Z \over 4\pi} \Re(\Delta_Z(s))
\sqrt{1 - 4\rho}{1 + 2\rho \over 3}
.
\ee

\P
Calculations of the terms in~\rf{chargeCross} that involve scalar neutrinos
is more complicated due to the integrations over $\Delta_{\tilde\nu}(t)$-like
terms. By the equation~\rf{charge-d-nu-nu}

\bem
\sigma^T_{\tilde{\nu}\tilde{\nu}} & = &
{m_e^2 m_\chi^2 \over \pi s^2} \int_{t_-}^{t_+} X_{\tilde\nu\tilde\nu} dt
\nel
&\approx &

{g^4 \cos^4\theta_X\over 64 \pi s^2}
\int_{t_-}^{t_+} \abs{\Delta_{\tilde\nu}(t)}^2(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2 dt
\nel
& = &
{g^4 \cos^4\theta_X\over 64 \pi s^2}
\int_{t_-}^{t_+} 
{1 \over \abs{t - x}^2}[t - s(\rho - 1)]^2
dt.
\ee

According to~\rf{csInt65} and from the definition~\rf{charge-x-is} of $x$ I get
\beml{charge-total-nu-nu}
\sigma^T_{\tilde{\nu}\tilde{\nu}} & \approx  &
{g^4 \cos^4\theta_X\over 64 \pi s^2}
\Biggl\{
{[\Re(x) + s(1 - \rho)]^2 - \Im(x)^2 \over \Im(x)}
\phi(x)
\nel&&
\qquad{}
+ 2[\Re(x) + s(1 - \rho)]\ln\abs{t_+ - x \over t_- - x}
+ s\sqrt{1 - 4\rho}
\Biggr\}
\nel & \equiv  &
{g^4 \cos^4\theta_X\over 64 \pi s^2}
\Biggl\{
-{[m_{\tilde{\nu}}^2 + s(1 - \rho)]^2 
    - m_{\tilde{\nu}}^2\Gamma_{\tilde{\nu}}^2 
  \over m_{\tilde{\nu}}\Gamma_{\tilde{\nu}}}
\phi(x)
\nel&&
\qquad{}
+ 2[m_{\tilde{\nu}}^2 + s(1 - \rho)]\ln\abs{t_+ - x \over t_- - x}
+ s\sqrt{1 - 4\rho}
\Biggr\}
,
\ee

where $\phi(x)$ is defined in~\rf{csImLog}. In this case it becomes
\bem
\phi(x) & = & \arctg{s\sqrt{1 - 4\rho}\Im(x)
         \over m_\chi^4 + \abs{x}^2 - s(2\rho - 1)\Re(x)}
\nel
& \equiv &          
-
\arctg{s\sqrt{1 - 4\rho}\, m_{\tilde{\nu}}\Gamma_{\tilde{\nu}}
         \over s^2 \rho^2 
            + m_{\tilde{\nu}}^2[m_{\tilde{\nu}}^2 + \Gamma_{\tilde{\nu}}^2
             + s(1 - 2\rho)]}
.
\ee

\P
Finally I consider the interference between the bosons and sneutrino channels.
To find $\sigma^T_{\gamma\tilde{\nu}}$ from~\rf{charge-d-gamma-nu} 
I apply~\rf{csInt3} and~\rf{csInt4} 
in the case when $z = 1$, so
\bem
\sigma^T_{\gamma\tilde{\nu}}
& = &
{m_e^2 m_\chi^2 \over \pi s^2} \int_{t_-}^{t_+} X_{\gamma\tilde\nu} dt
\nel
& \approx &
-{e^2 g^2 \cos^2\theta_X \over 32\pi s^3 }
\int_{t_-}^{t_+} 
\Re[\Delta_{\tilde{\nu}}(t)]
\left[(s + t - m_\chi^2)^2 + s m_\chi^2\right]
dt
\nel
& = &
-{e^2 g^2 \cos^2\theta_X \over 32\pi s^3 }
\int_{t_-}^{t_+} 
\Re\left({ 1 \over t - x}\right)
\left\{[t - s(\rho - 1)]^2 + s m_\chi^2\right\}
dt
\nel
& = &
-
{e^2 g^2 \cos^2\theta_X \over 32\pi s^3 }
\Biggl\{
\Re[(x - s(\rho - 1))^2] \ln\abs{t_+ - x \over t_- - x}
\nel
&&\qquad{}
+ \Im[((x - s(\rho - 1))^2] \phi(x)
\nel
&&\qquad{}
+ s\sqrt{1 - 4\rho}[\Re(x) - 2s(\rho - 1) - s(1 - 2\rho)/2]
+ s^2\rho\ln\abs{t_+ - x \over t_- - x}
\Biggr\}
,
\ee

Thus
from~\rf{csReImForSub} I have,

\beml{charge-total-gamma-nu}
\sigma^T_{\gamma\tilde{\nu}} & \approx  &
-{e^2 g^2 \cos^2\theta_X \over 32\pi s^3 }
\Biggl\{
[(\Re(x) + s(1 - \rho))^2 - \Im(x)^2 + s^2\rho] 
\ln\abs{t_+ - x \over t_- - x}
\nel
&&\qquad{}
- 2\Im(x)[\Re(x) + s(1 - \rho)] \phi(x)

+ s\sqrt{1 - 4\rho}[\Re(x) - s\rho + {3 \over 2}s]
\Biggr\}
\nel
& \equiv &
-{e^2 g^2 \cos^2\theta_X \over 32\pi s^3 }
\Biggl\{
[(m_{\tilde\nu}^2 + s(1 - \rho))^2 
- m_{\tilde\nu}^2\Gamma_{\tilde\nu}^2 + s^2\rho] 
\ln\abs{t_+ - x \over t_- - x}
\nel
&&\qquad{}
+ 2m_{\tilde\nu}\Gamma_{\tilde\nu}[m_{\tilde\nu}^2 + s(1 - \rho)] \phi(x)

+ s\sqrt{1 - 4\rho}[m_{\tilde\nu}^2 - s\rho + {3 \over 2}s]
\Biggr\}
.
\ee

To calculate $\sigma^T_{Z\tilde{\nu}}$ I rewrite it from~\rf{charge-d-Z-nu} as
\beml{charge-total-Z-nu}
\sigma^T_{Z\tilde{\nu}} & = & 
{m_e^2 m_\chi^2 \over \pi s^2} \int_{t_-}^{t_+} X_{Z\tilde\nu} dt
\nel
& \approx &
{g^4 \tan^2 \theta_W \cos^2\theta_X \over 64 \pi s^2}
\int_{t_-}^{t_+} \Re\left[\Delta_Z^\hc(s) { 1 \over t - x}\right]
\nel &&{}\times
\Bigl\{
[t - s(\rho - 1)]^2[\cos(2\theta_W) + \cos^2\theta_Y]
+
s^2\rho[\cos(2\theta_W) + \cos^2\theta_X]
\Bigr\}
dt
\nel
& = &
{g^4 \tan^2 \theta_W \cos^2\theta_X \over 64 \pi}
\{f_1[\cos(2\theta_W) + \cos^2\theta_Y]
    + f_2[\cos(2\theta_W) + \cos^2\theta_X]\}
.
\nel
\ee

The formulas~\rf{csInt3} and~\rf{csInt4} give for $f_1$ and $f_2$,


\bem
f_1 & = & {1 \over s^2}\int_{t_-}^{t_+}
 \Re\left[\Delta_Z^\hc(s){ 1 \over t - x}\right][t - s(\rho - 1)]^2 dt
\nel
& = &
\Re\{z[x/s + 1 - \rho]^2\} \ln\abs{t_+ - x \over t_- - x}
-\Im\{z[x/s + 1 - \rho]\} \phi(x)
\nel
&&{}
+ {1 \over s}
  \sqrt{1 - 4\rho}\{\Re(zx) - \Re(z)[2s(\rho - 1) + s(1 - 2\rho)/2]\}
\nel
& = &
\Re\{z[x/s + 1 - \rho]^2\} \ln\abs{t_+ - x \over t_- - x}
-\Im\{z[x/s + 1 - \rho]^2\} \phi(x)
\nel
&&{}
+ \sqrt{1 - 4\rho}[\Re(zx/s) + ({3\over 2} - \rho)\Re(z)]
,
\nel
f_2 & = & \rho \int_{t_-}^{t_+} 
 \Re\left[\Delta_Z^\hc(s){ 1 \over t - x}\right] dt
=
\rho\left[\Re(z)\ln\abs{t_+ - x \over t_- - x}
- \Im(z) \phi(x)\right],
\ee

where  
\be
z = \Delta_Z^\hc(s) = {1 \over s - \mB^2 - i\mB\Gamma_Z}
.
\ee
